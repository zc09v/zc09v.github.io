LocalVault — Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 2026 · Last Updated: May 2026

1. Our Commitment to Privacy

LocalVault is designed from the ground up with privacy as its foundation. Your data belongs to you. We cannot see it, cannot access it, and cannot transmit it — because LocalVault has no network capability whatsoever.

This policy describes how LocalVault handles your information. It is intentionally short because there is very little to disclose.

2. Data Collection and Storage

2.1 What We Collect

Nothing. LocalVault does not collect, transmit, or store any of your personal information on external servers. The app does not:

  • Track your usage or behavior
  • Collect analytics or diagnostic data
  • Record crash logs (unless you explicitly opt in via system-level crash reporting)
  • Use any form of telemetry
  • Send data to any remote server

2.2 Where Your Data Lives

All data you create within LocalVault — including passwords, usernames, URLs, and notes — is stored exclusively on your device, inside the app's local sandbox container.

No data leaves your device at any time, for any reason.

3. Encryption

3.1 Field-Level Encryption

Each password stored in LocalVault is individually encrypted (via Apple's native CryptoKit framework) before being written to the local database. The encryption key is derived by a memory-hard key derivation function resistant to GPU/ASIC attacks.

3.2 Master Password

Your master password is never stored in plaintext. Instead, a verification token (encrypted with your master-password-derived key) is stored in the system Keychain. When you unlock LocalVault, your entered password is used to attempt to decrypt this token — if it succeeds, the password is correct.

3.3 Export Security

LocalVault supports exporting your data as an encrypted .crhino file for backup or migration. Plaintext CSV export is also available but requires an explicit security warning confirmation.

4. Third-Party Services

LocalVault does not use any third-party services, analytics frameworks, or advertising SDKs.

The app uses open-source libraries that execute entirely on your device and do not communicate with any external server.

5. Network Access

LocalVault is a zero-network application. The entitlements file explicitly disables outgoing network connections. The app is built without URLSession, WebSocket, or any other networking framework.

The sole exception is that you may choose to click a URL stored in an entry or visit this privacy policy, which will open the URL in your default web browser via the system API. This action is entirely user-initiated and delegates the network request to your browser. LocalVault itself does not make any network request.

6. App Sandbox

LocalVault runs inside Apple's App Sandbox, which restricts the app's ability to access files or resources outside its container. The sandbox requires explicit user permission for file operations like import and export.

7. Data Sharing

We do not share, sell, rent, or trade your data with anyone — because we never have access to it in the first place.

LocalVault does not sync with iCloud, Dropbox, or any cloud service. Your encrypted .crhino export files, if you choose to create them, are stored at a location of your choosing and remain under your sole control.

8. Children's Privacy

LocalVault is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

9. Your Control

You have full control over your data at all times:

  • Delete individual entries within the app at any time
  • Reset your account from Settings to wipe all data, encryption keys, and Keychain credentials
  • Export your data in encrypted (.crhino) or plaintext (.csv) format for migration
  • Delete the app — which removes the sandbox container and all data with it

10. Changes to This Policy

If we make material changes to this policy, we will update the effective date above. Since LocalVault has no network access, you will be informed of changes through App Store release notes.
